Best Practices Library
01
The 5-Second Homepage Test: Does Your Site Pass?
CRO 4 min read Popular
Your homepage has roughly 5 seconds to communicate three things: what you sell, why it matters, and what to do next. Run this quick test. Load your site on a phone and hand it to someone who has never seen it. After 5 seconds, take it back and ask them what the brand does. If they cannot answer clearly, your homepage is leaking conversions. Focus on a single hero message, one clear CTA, and social proof above the fold.
02
Why Your Welcome Flow Needs 5 Emails, Not 3
Email 3 min read
Most brands send a welcome discount and two follow-ups, then stop. That leaves money on the table. A proper 5-email welcome flow builds the relationship: immediate delivery of the incentive, brand story, social proof, education about your hero product, and a final urgency nudge. Each email has a distinct job. Your Email Strategist agent can build this entire sequence. Just ask.
03
The Creative Fatigue Formula: When to Refresh Your Ads
Ads 5 min read
Creative fatigue is the silent killer of ad performance. Watch for these signals: frequency above 3.0, click-through rate dropping 20%+ week on week, and CPM rising while conversion rate falls. When two of these three conditions hold for 3+ days, it is time to rotate new creative. Your Ad Creative Agent tracks these metrics and flags refresh points automatically.
04
How to Read Your Weekly Agent Report (and What to Do With It)
Strategy 6 min read Essential
Every Monday morning, your agents compile a weekly brief. Here is how to read it in under 5 minutes. Start with the Executive Summary at the top: it highlights the single biggest win and the one item that needs your attention. Then scan the KPI table for any red arrows. Finally, review the Recommended Actions section. Each action has an "Approve" button. Click it to greenlight the agent, or flag it for your next strategy call.
05
Mobile Checkout: The 7 Friction Points Killing Your Conversion
CRO 4 min read
Over 70% of your traffic is mobile, but mobile checkout conversion is typically half of desktop. The seven friction points to fix: (1) too many form fields, (2) no guest checkout, (3) hidden shipping costs, (4) no express payment options, (5) small tap targets, (6) slow page loads, and (7) missing trust badges. Fix these and you can expect a 15-25% lift in mobile conversion rate.
06
Building a Content Calendar That Actually Gets Executed
Content 5 min read
Most content calendars fail because they are too ambitious. Start with 2 pieces per week: one SEO-driven blog article and one social post repurposed from it. Your Content Strategist agent creates the briefs, your SEO agent optimises them, and your Social agent handles distribution. The key is consistency over volume. After 90 days, review what performed and double down.
07
The ROAS Threshold: When to Scale and When to Pull Back
Ads 4 min read
Not all ROAS is created equal. Your breakeven ROAS depends on your margins, not an arbitrary target. Calculate it: 1 divided by your gross margin percentage. If your margin is 60%, your breakeven ROAS is 1.67x. Anything above that is profitable. Scale campaigns at 20% budget increases every 3 days when ROAS exceeds your threshold by 30%+. Pull back when ROAS drops below breakeven for 5+ consecutive days.
08
Setting Up Your Weekly Growth Rhythm
Strategy 3 min read Essential
Growth compounds when it becomes a habit. Here is the weekly rhythm we recommend. Monday: review your agent brief (5 min). Tuesday: approve or adjust recommended actions (10 min). Wednesday: check ad performance midweek (5 min). Thursday: review any new content or email drafts (10 min). Friday: update priorities for next week (5 min). Total time: 35 minutes per week. Your agents handle the rest.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I check the dashboard?

▼
We recommend checking your dashboard twice a week: once on Monday to review your weekly agent brief, and once midweek (Wednesday or Thursday) for a quick performance check. This takes about 10 minutes total. Your agents will send you automatic alerts if anything urgent needs attention between check-ins, so you never need to worry about missing something critical.

Can I change which agents are active?

▼
Yes. Your core agents are always active by default, but you can activate or deactivate specialist agents at any time from the Agent Team page. Simply click on any agent card and toggle their status. Keep in mind that some specialists activate automatically based on triggers (for example, the Cart Recovery Specialist activates when abandonment rates exceed your threshold). Your strategist can help you configure the right mix for your current goals.

How do I know if my agents are performing well?

▼
Each agent has a performance score visible on their card in the Agent Team page. Scores above 85% mean the agent is performing excellently. Between 70-85% is solid. Below 70% means the agent may need updated instructions or additional data. Your weekly brief also highlights any underperforming agents with specific recommendations. The Reports section gives you a deeper breakdown of each agent's impact on your key metrics.

What happens if an agent makes a mistake?

▼
Agents do not publish or execute anything without your approval for high-impact actions. All ad spend changes, email sends, and website modifications require your sign-off first. For lower-impact tasks like drafting content or generating reports, agents work autonomously but flag anything outside normal parameters. If you spot an error, click "Flag Issue" on the task card and your strategist will be notified immediately. The agent will pause that task stream until the issue is resolved.

How do I request something specific from an agent?

▼
Use the Task Assignment feature on the Agent Team page. Select the relevant agent (or let the system route it automatically), describe what you need in plain English, set a priority level, and submit. Your agent will acknowledge the task within minutes and provide an estimated completion time. For complex requests, you can also raise them during your weekly strategy call and your human strategist will coordinate the agents on your behalf.

When should I escalate to my human strategist?

▼
Escalate when you need strategic direction, not tactical execution. Good reasons to reach out: planning a product launch, responding to a competitor move, adjusting your overall growth strategy, or when you disagree with an agent's recommendation. Your strategist is also the right contact for budget discussions, channel prioritisation, and creative direction. Day-to-day tasks (writing copy, analysing data, optimising campaigns) should go to your agents.

Can I pause agents during quiet periods?

▼
Absolutely. If you have a seasonal quiet period or want to pause specific activities, you can put individual agents or entire teams on standby from the Agent Team page. Agents on standby stop executing tasks but continue monitoring your metrics and will alert you to any significant changes. This is useful during stock shortages, holiday closures, or while you are reformulating your product range. Reactivation takes effect immediately.

How do I upgrade my tier?

▼
Tier upgrades unlock additional agents, higher task limits, and priority support. To upgrade, go to Settings in the sidebar and select "Plan and Billing". You will see a comparison of your current tier versus available upgrades. Alternatively, mention it during your next strategy call and your strategist will walk you through the options and recommend the right fit based on your current growth stage and goals. Upgrades take effect at the start of your next billing cycle, or immediately if you prefer.
